NYCHA Coastal Resiliency

In October 2012, the storm surge from Superstorm Sandy impacted 10% of the New York Housing Authority's (NYCHA) low- and moderate-income developments. Hundreds of buildings were left without power, heat, and hot water for several months. NYCHA sustained over $3 billion in damage.

This map displays NYCHA properties and populations in relation to 100- and 500-Year floodplains based on 2020 projections. Hover over a development to see its name and population. Toggle between the 100- and 500-year floodplains to see which developments will be impacted by future storm surge.
